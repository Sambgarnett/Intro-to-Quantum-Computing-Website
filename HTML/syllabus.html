Course Overview

Intro to Quantum Computing intends to give anyone interested an introduction to quantum computing, no background needed! Intro to Quantum Computing builds off of computing (CSCI-2200) and algorithm (CSCI-2300) basics. This course delves into fundemental principles like qubits, superposition, entanglement, and quantum circuits/algorithms. In this course, students will engage in hands-on lab sessions using Qiskit/OpenQASM and/or AWS/Azure cloud services, exploring both quantum circuits and algorithms. Students will also focus on classical simulation of quantum circuits, exploring ways machine learning can be used in classical optimization, with discussion on current events in quantum computing regarding IBM and Google.

This course approaches Quatum Computing with a linear algebrea basis. This course aims to produce Quantum-aware engineers via lectures and Quantum-proficent engineers via labs. Upon completion of this course, students will have a solid grasp of quantum computing principles, an understanding of quantum advantage, proficency in reading and analyzing quantum circuits and algorithms, and insight into practical applications.
