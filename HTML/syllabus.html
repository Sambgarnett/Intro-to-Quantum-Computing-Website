Syllabus

Course Overview

Intro to Quantum Computing intends to give anyone interested an introduction to quantum computing, no background needed! Intro to Quantum Computing builds off of computing (CSCI-2200) and algorithm (CSCI-2300) basics. This course delves into fundamental principles like qubits, superposition, entanglement, and quantum circuits/algorithms. In this course, students will engage in hands-on lab sessions using Qiskit/OpenQASM and/or AWS/Azure cloud services, exploring both quantum circuits and algorithms. Students will also focus on classical simulation of quantum circuits, exploring ways machine learning can be used in classical optimization, with discussion on current events in quantum computing regarding IBM and Google.

This course approaches Quantum Computing with a linear algebra basis. This course aims to produce Quantum-aware engineers via lectures and Quantum-proficient engineers via labs. Upon completion of this course, students will have a solid grasp of quantum computing principles, an understanding of quantum advantage, proficiency in reading and analyzing quantum circuits and algorithms, and insight into practical applications.

Course Topics

Part 0 - Quantum Computing Theory and Mathmatics

  • Algebra, Complex Numbers, and Triginometry
  • Quantum Information with Single Qubits: Qubits, Bloch Sphere, and Quantum Gates
  • Complex Quantum Circuits, Linear Algebra, and Tensor Networks
  • Quantum Information of Multiple-Qubit Systems: Density Matricies and Block Ball
  • Languges and Turing Machine

Part 1 - Quantum Algorithms

  • P, NP, and Quantum Speedups
  • Quantum Supremacy and Empirical Quantum Advantages
  • Shor's Algorithm
  • Grover's Algorithm
  • Quantum Approximation Optimization Algorithm (QAOA)
  • Quantum Error Correction and NISQ

Part 2 - Quantum Applications

  • Quantum Optimization
  • Quantum Machine Learning
  • Quantum Simulation for Physical, Chemical, and Biological Systems
  • AI + Quantum Computing
  • Finance + Quantum Computing
  • Material Science + Quantum Computing

Part 3 - Lab Sessions and Projects

  • Lab 1: IBM Quantum Learning Suite, Example of GHZ Gate
  • Lab 2: Google's Sycamore Circuits
  • Lab 3: Image Classification (quantum machine learning)
  • Lab 4: Quantum Basic Linear Algebra Subroutines (qBLAS)
  • Project 1: Finding the Ground State of Ising model Using Learning-to-Anneal Algorithm
  • Project 2: Quantum approximate optimization algorithm (QAOA)
  • Project 3: Challenging the Claim of Quantum Supremacy
